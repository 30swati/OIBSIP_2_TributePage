APJ Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015) was an Indian aaerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002-2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He is known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Time - Line

1950-1954: Pursues a degree in Physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. Known for his hard work and dedication to his studies.

1954-1955:Applies for the Indian Air Force but is not selected. Instead, he moves to Madras and joins the Madras Institute of Technology to study aeronautical engineering.

1958: Graduates with a degree in aeronautical engineering. Begins working as a scientist at the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the DRDO.

1963-1969: Plays a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and the country's first satellite, named Aryabhata.

1970s: Takes up various roles within the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and contributes significantly to the development of satellite launch vehicles.

1980s: Leads the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), resulting in the successful development of missiles such as Agni and Prithvi.

1992-1997: Appointed as the Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister and later as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister. Advocates for technology self-reliance and a strong defense.

1997: Receives the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, for his contributions to the country's defense and space programs.

2002-2007: Serves as the 11th President of India. Focuses on inspiring the youth, promoting education, and addressing societal issues.

Post-Presidency: Devotes his time to education and writing. Interacts extensively with students, delivering lectures and encouraging innovation.

27 July 2015: Dies of a heart attack while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam's life is characterized by his unwavering commitment to science, education, and national development. His humble beginnings, combined with his intellect and dedication, make him a revered figure in Indian history
